## Phase 3 Signature Moments (Levels 4-10)

This design map defines the distinct encounter identity for each remaining campaign level.

### Level 4 — Fracture Convoy
- **Signature:** "Crossfire-to-corridor command squeeze"
- **Identity cue:** Alternating line-breaking ring sweeps and mine pockets, then a convoy lock tunnel.
- **Key hazard blend:** `ring-crossfire` + `minefield` + `rock-corridor`.

### Level 5 — Cinder Vault
- **Signature:** "Heat ladder with ignition breaks"
- **Identity cue:** Progressive heat climb where storm spikes interrupt otherwise stable lanes.
- **Key hazard blend:** `debris-surge` + `energy-storm` + short corridor pressure.

### Level 6 — Graveyard Lattice
- **Signature:** "Salvage-lane choreography"
- **Identity cue:** Wreck lanes collapse/reopen and force route planning under mine disruption.
- **Key hazard blend:** `rock-corridor` + `minefield` + scrap burst timing.

### Level 7 — Mirage Archive
- **Signature:** "Deceptive calm ambush memory trap"
- **Identity cue:** Calm windows are repeatedly broken by directional ambushes and false-safe pockets.
- **Key hazard blend:** `nebula-ambush` + `gravity-well` + deceptive `minefield`.

### Level 8 — Halo Cartography
- **Signature:** "Predictive orbital cadence"
- **Identity cue:** Repeatable geometric ring patterns reward forward prediction, not raw reaction.
- **Key hazard blend:** dominant `ring-crossfire` cycles with selective gravity/disruption punctuation.

### Level 9 — Glass Rift Narrows
- **Signature:** "Claustrophobic lockjaw gauntlet"
- **Identity cue:** Canyon narrowing and lane seal moments where micro-corrections decide survival.
- **Key hazard blend:** heavy `rock-corridor` with debris/gravity trap follow-through.

### Level 10 — Eventide Singularity
- **Signature:** "Convergence overload"
- **Identity cue:** Final exam where multiple high-threat systems overlap in controlled waves.
- **Key hazard blend:** layered `ring-crossfire` + `gravity-well` + `energy-storm` + debris punctuation.

## Implementation rules for this pass

- Keep each level's signature hazard blend unique relative to adjacent levels.
- Preserve readability: challenge should come from overlap and timing, not ambiguity.
- Keep boss-approach sections as intentional anticipation beats, not full-pressure carryover.
