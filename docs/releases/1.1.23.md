# Release 1.1.23 — chart the safe convergence corridor

Close the audit findings that could let pause state, helper contact, narrow upgrade layouts, or final-hazard pressure drift outside the authored route.

## Highlights

- Keeps respawn completion from resuming Arcade physics while another gameplay pause source is still active.
- Adds contact-specific helper-wing damage pacing so enemy overlap cannot drain helpers every frame, while projectile and bomb damage stay immediate.
- Makes PlanetIntermission upgrade buttons fit narrow portrait and short landscape viewports with a compact single-column fallback.
- Adds validation for hazard pressure reachability and retunes Eventide convergence so every authored hazard grammar can actually fire.
- Expands static hygiene around tests/scripts and syncs docs with current Phaser/package/bundle defaults.

## Lore

Constraint: Preserve GameScene transition ownership, PauseStateController pause authority, helper-wing direct-damage semantics, and the existing global hazard pressure model.
Rejected: Resume physics directly from respawn callbacks | The pause controller may still be holding orientation/manual pause state, so GameScene must gate resume through current gameplay-pause state.
Rejected: Add a global helper damage cooldown | Projectile and bomb hits are discrete authored damage sources; only enemy body contact needed overlap pacing.
Rejected: Loosen hazard pressure globally | The starvation was authored cadence/order pressure in Eventide convergence, not a reason to weaken the campaign-wide throttling contract.
Confidence: high
Scope-risk: medium
Directive: Keep terminal convergence readable by making every authored hazard reachable, and keep recovery paths subordinate to active pause state.
